Fictional teaching case. A 70-year-old patient with known severe aortic stenosis presents with chest pain and acute shortness of breath. Examination and imaging reveal pulmonary edema. Troponin is elevated and cardiac catheterization has been performed for suspected acute coronary syndrome.

A pulmonary artery catheter is being floated in the CCU to guide management of cardiogenic shock complicated by severe aortic stenosis.
